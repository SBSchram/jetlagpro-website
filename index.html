The Science Behind Jet Lag Pro

What is Jet Lag?

Jet lag happens when you travel to a different time zone and your body gets confused. You might feel tired, sick to your stomach, get headaches, have trouble thinking clearly, or struggle to sleep properly. Sound familiar? You're not alone - millions of travelers deal with this every year.

Your Body's Internal Clock

Your body runs on natural rhythms, just like the sun rising and setting each day. Scientists call this your "circadian rhythm" - basically your internal 24-hour clock.1 This clock controls when you feel sleepy, when you're alert, and many other body functions.2 Even your individual cells have their own timekeepers!3 The most obvious example is your sleep-wake cycle.

Why Jet Lag Happens

Jet lag occurs when your internal clock gets out of sync with the new time zone. Here's what happens: your brain produces a hormone called melatonin that makes you sleepy when it gets dark. When you travel across time zones, your body is still producing melatonin based on your home time, not your destination time.

Simply put: jet lag is when your body clock doesn't match the actual time at your destination.

How Does Chinese Medicine Help?

For thousands of years, Chinese medicine has understood that our bodies follow natural rhythms.4 According to this ancient wisdom, your body has 12 different organ systems, and each one is most active during a specific 2-hour window throughout the day. Think of it like a 24-hour schedule where different parts of your body "take their turn" being the most active.

The 24-Hour Meridian Clock

Modern science has proven that acupuncture actually works! Research shows that stimulating certain points can reset your body's internal clock by affecting the genes that control your daily rhythms.5 The question is: how can regular travelers use this knowledge to beat jet lag?

The Breakthrough Discovery

In 1978, a researcher named Willem Khoe had a brilliant idea: what if you could use specific acupressure points to help reset your body clock for jet lag?6 Other experts like John Amaro built on this concept and developed it further.78 Today, JetLagPro puts this proven approach right in your pocket.

Putting Science into Practice

The research is clear: targeting specific acupressure points based on your destination's time can help reset your body clock. While you could manually calculate time zones and look up point locations yourself, that's exactly the kind of tedious work that keeps people from trying this proven approach.
