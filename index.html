What is Jet Lag?

Jet lag happens when you travel to a different time zone and your body gets confused.

üî¨ The Science

Your body runs on natural rhythms, just like the sun rising and setting each day. Scientists call this your "circadian rhythm" - basically your internal 24-hour clock.1 This clock controls when you feel sleepy, when you're alert, and many other body functions.2 Even your individual cells have their own timekeepers!3

Why Jet Lag Happens

Your internal clock gets out of sync with the new time zone. Your brain produces melatonin based on your home time, not your destination time.

üí° Simply Put

Jet lag is when your body clock doesn't match the actual time at your destination.
