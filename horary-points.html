The Chinese Organ Clock in Everyday Terms

Chinese medicine describes a 24-hour cycle where different organ systems take turns being "on duty." Each two-hour window is linked to a specific active meridian.

For each of these twelve meridians, there is one ideal point (the "horary point"). In JetLagPro we also focus on the horary point but may shift to an active point that you can actually reach during a flight.

The app always knows which meridian is active at your destination time and shows you the best point to use based on your seating option. With "Cramped seating" you get points that are easy to reach; otherwise you'll be guided to the classical point.

The Chinese Organ Clock Wheel

The wheel shows the 24-hour day divided into twelve two-hour slices. Each slice belongs to one meridian and has a single key point that JetLagPro uses for that active window.

The 12 Active Meridian Windows

The JetLagPro method uses one ideal point for each two-hour meridian window, always tied to the meridian that is active at that time. In some windows, the app may show a substitute point on the same meridian when circumstances (like cramped seating) make the ideal point hard to reach. The app and the website demo show you exactly where each point is and how to stimulate it.

How This Works for Jet Lag

When you travel across time zones, your body's internal clock (circadian rhythm) remains synchronized with your departure time zone. By stimulating the horary points associated with your destination time zone, you're essentially "telling" your body what time it should be at your destination.

For example, if you're flying from New York to London (5-hour time difference), and it's 3:00 PM in New York but 8:00 PM in London, you would stimulate the point that's active at 8:00 PM (Pericardium-8) rather than the point active at 3:00 PM (Small Intestine-5).

This technique helps your body begin the process of adjusting to the new time zone before you even arrive, potentially reducing the severity and duration of jet lag symptoms.

Standard vs Cramped-Seating Points

In the classical lists, several time-linked points are on the feet or behind the knees. On an airplane, especially in a cramped seat, those spots can be hard or awkward to reach without twisting or climbing around other passengers.

JetLagPro always stays with the same active meridian and time window, but when you turn on the Cramped seating option the app switches to a more accessible point on that meridian. The app and demo show you exactly which points are used in each setting.
