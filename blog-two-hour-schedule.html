How the JetLagPro 2-Hour Schedule Works

May 30, 2026 • By Dr. Steven Schram, Licensed Acupuncturist

Chronoacupuncture is not “one point before bed.” It is a sequence tied to destination local time: roughly every two hours, you stimulate the horary point for whichever meridian is “on duty” on the Chinese Organ Clock. JetLagPro automates that math. Here is exactly what the app does and how to follow it on a real trip.

Step 1: Choose Your Destination

When you select an arrival airport, JetLagPro sets your schedule to that city’s time zone. All reminders and on-screen points use destination time, not the time at your departure city. That is the core rule of the method: you are teaching your body what “now” means where you are going.

You can start the guide before boarding, during the flight, or after landing. Many travelers begin at the airport so the first destination-time windows are already in motion when wheels go up.

Step 2: The 12 Two-Hour Windows

Traditional horary theory divides the day into twelve blocks of about two hours. Each block belongs to one meridian (Lung, Large Intestine, Stomach, and so on through the cycle). Each meridian has a primary horary point—the point considered most responsive during that window.

JetLagPro shows one active point at a time: the point for the meridian whose window matches current destination time. When the clock crosses into the next two-hour slice, the app advances to the next point in the cycle.

See the full list and images in The Active Meridian Points. For background on the clock itself, read What Is Chronoacupuncture?

Step 3: Stimulate When Prompted (Both Sides)

When a reminder arrives—or when you open the app on your own—use firm pressure or quick tapping on the point shown. Classical instructions usually include both the left and right sides of the body for that point.

Technique varies by teacher: some use a fingertip, others a pen cap or similar tool for 20–30 seconds of repeated taps. JetLagPro’s videos demonstrate a practical approach; the research study records which points you marked, not how many seconds you pressed.

Step 4: Repeat for About 24 Hours

The point guide runs for roughly one full day on destination time—long enough to cover each meridian window at least once through travel and the first day on the ground. The guide ends when:

  • The last scheduled transition completes,
  • You stop the guide manually, or
  • The ~24-hour timeout is reached.

This matches how acupuncturists have taught the jet-lag protocol for decades: not a single session, but a repeating rhythm aligned to the new zone.

What If You Miss a Window?

Sleep, meals, and movie marathons happen. If you miss one or more two-hour blocks:

  • Do not try to “catch up” by doing every skipped point in a row.
  • Pick up with the point for the current destination time when you wake or check the app.

Horary logic cares about which meridian is active now, not about replaying the entire past day. JetLagPro’s active point display always reflects current destination time so you can resume without manual calculation.

Ideal Points vs. Cramped-Seat Mode

Five of the classical horary points sit on the feet or behind the knees— awkward in a middle seat. In the app’s Info tab, Cramped seating points swaps in reachable alternatives on the same active meridian while keeping the same two-hour timing.

Turn cramped mode off when you have space to use the ideal points (for example at home after arrival). Timing stays identical; only the anatomical location changes.

How Reminders Fit In

On iPhone and Apple Watch, JetLagPro can notify you when a new point becomes active. Notifications follow the same destination-time schedule as the on-screen guide— typically at odd-hour boundaries in the destination zone (the hand-off between meridian windows).

Android users can use the website interactive demo or PWA-style reminders where supported; the native iOS app is the primary product during the research phase.

How This Differs From a Static Chart

Paper jet-lag charts ask you to read a table and do time-zone arithmetic while jet-lagged. JetLagPro:

  • Resolves airport → time zone automatically
  • Highlights the current point only
  • Advances the schedule as destination midnight rolls forward (including day-boundary edge cases)
  • Offers video, timers, and Watch sync on iOS

Research and Expectations

JetLagPro is free during an observational study of real-world use and jet lag symptoms. The schedule implements a traditional protocol; it does not guarantee a particular recovery speed.
