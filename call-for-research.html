Help Study Timed Acupressure for Jet Lag

Research recruitment · Steven Schram, PhD, DC, LAc · Plain-language summary

JetLagPro is a free iPhone app that reminds you to press specific acupressure points on a schedule set to your destination time zone. You use finger pressure only—no needles.

We are running an observational research study to learn whether using the app on real trips is linked to how travelers report jet lag symptoms afterward. Participation in the research upload is optional, anonymous, and requires your in-app consent after each qualifying trip.

Who can take part

  • Adults who travel across time zones
  • English-language research materials (consent and survey)
  • An iPhone (Apple Watch is optional)

What you would do

  1. Install the app from jetlagpro.com (TestFlight beta until the App Store link is live).
  2. Use it on a real trip—pick your destination and follow the timed point reminders for up to about 24 hours.
  3. Choose whether to share data for research. After a qualifying trip, the app shows an informed-consent form. Tap Share only if you agree; tap Not now if you do not. The app still works either way.
  4. Optional survey about two days after the trip (~5 minutes), if you shared trip data.

What we collect (only if you tap Share)

Anonymous trip details (routes, time zones, which points you marked) and optional survey answers about jet lag symptoms. We do not collect your name, email, or phone number in the research database.

Time, cost, and rewards

  • Time: reminders for about 24 hours during travel, plus an optional short survey later.
  • Cost: the app is free. There are no upgrades or ads.
  • Payment or lifetime access: none. We do not offer money, gifts, or free lifetime access for research participation.

Voluntary

You may stop sharing research data at any time in the app. Past anonymous uploads cannot be linked back to you for deletion.

For licensed acupuncturists

Historical articles (Khoe, Amaro) sometimes use the term chronoacupuncture for timed point work during travel. This study uses self-administered acupressure only—not needle acupuncture. The schedule follows horary timing on the Chinese Organ Clock, with a few point substitutions for economy-class accessibility (see the twelve points).

We are not claiming that a formal power analysis has been completed. The study is pre-registered on OSF and aims to describe patterns in real-world use toward roughly 500 consented qualifying trips—an observational association study, not a claim of proven treatment benefit.
